# Pitch Deck Outline

## 1. Title

Gesellig: turning free moments into shared activities.

## 2. Problem

Traditional loneliness outreach reaches a self-selected minority. People who most need connection often avoid initiatives that label them as lonely.

## 3. Insight

The barrier is often coordination, not willingness: shared interests, overlapping time, safe places, and comfort boundaries are hard to line up.

## 4. Solution

Gesellig is an opt-in activity planner for residents. It suggests real-life activities based on interests, availability, comfort preferences, and rough location.

## 5. Product Flow

- Alias profile
- Interests and comfort settings
- Availability and routines
- Three transparent suggestions
- Safe public meetup
- Feedback and repeat-meet signal

## 6. AI Positioning

Core message:

> We do not use AI to detect loneliness. We use AI to reduce the coordination friction that prevents real-life connection.

Use transparent rule-based matching in the prototype. Future AI can improve coordination, copy, and planning only under strict consent and privacy constraints.

## 7. Safety and Dignity

- No loneliness score
- No mental-health inference
- Alias-first onboarding
- Public-place defaults
- Report/block/cancel controls
- Group or hosted settings by default
- Municipality sees aggregate metrics only

## 8. Municipal Dashboard

Show impact without individual surveillance:

- Accepted suggestions
- Completed meetups
- Repeat meetups
- Completion rate
- Connection pulse
- Popular activity types
- Neighborhood coverage with threshold suppression

## 9. Rollout

Pilot through low-stigma city and partner channels:

- Libraries
- Cafes
- Universities
- Sports clubs
- Museums
- Neighborhood pages
- Municipal newsletters

## 10. 18-Month Success Criteria

- Active residents
- Completed meetup rate
- Repeat meetup rate within 30 days
- Self-reported connection trend
- Opt-out and cancellation rate
- Neighborhood coverage
- Partner-hosted activity supply

## 11. Ask

Use seed funding to run an 18-month controlled municipal pilot across three Dutch cities with community partners, privacy review, and independent impact evaluation.
