# 3-Minute Demo Video Script

## Goal

Show that Gesellig helps cities enable real-world connection without diagnosing, scoring, or surveilling residents.

## Timing

### 0:00-0:20 — Problem

Voiceover:

"Many residents want more everyday connection, but they do not want to sign up for something labeled as a loneliness initiative. Gesellig reframes the problem: not as diagnosis, but as coordination."

Visual:

- Open on Gesellig welcome screen.
- Show Dutch flag and private-by-default line.

### 0:20-0:45 — Product Promise

Voiceover:

"We do not use AI to detect loneliness. We use AI to reduce the friction that stops real-life activities from happening: finding shared interests, overlapping time, public places, and comfort boundaries."

Visual:

- Tap "Start with an alias."
- Show alias and neighborhood screen.

### 0:45-1:20 — Resident Setup

Voiceover:

"A resident can use an alias, choose interests, and set comfort preferences. The experience feels like an activity planner, not a welfare intake."

Visual:

- Enter or keep alias.
- Choose walking, coffee, museums.
- Toggle small groups, public places, hosted events.

### 1:20-1:55 — Matching

Voiceover:

"Gesellig recommends only a few good options. Each recommendation explains why it fits, so residents understand the coordination logic."

Visual:

- Add Thursday evening availability.
- Show three suggestions.
- Open or focus on match reasons.
- Accept "Evening walk around Kralingse Plas."

### 1:55-2:25 — Safety and Feedback

Voiceover:

"First meetings default to safe public settings. Residents can cancel, report, or block. Afterward, feedback helps the system support longer-term connections."

Visual:

- Show confirmed activity screen.
- Point to public place, host, spots, cancel/report actions.
- Submit feedback and meet-again signal.

### 2:25-3:00 — Municipality Impact

Voiceover:

"Municipalities do not see individual profiles. They see aggregate outcomes: accepted suggestions, completed meetups, repeat meetups, connection pulse, and neighborhood coverage thresholds. That gives cities measurable impact without turning residents into surveillance targets."

Visual:

- Open dashboard.
- Show metrics and privacy guardrails.
- End on product promise.

## Recording Notes

- Keep phone viewport centered.
- Avoid showing browser chrome when possible.
- Use calm pace; no more than one full click every 2-3 seconds.
- Do not use words like "diagnose users" or "lonely residents" in the app walkthrough.
